Critical Precautions: Read Before Proceeding

Your 12- or 24-word mnemonic is the only unconditional backup of your private key.

Physical Backups are Mandatory — Write your seed words and BIP-39 passphrase onto physical media and store them in separate, secure locations. Never store these records digitally. If your USB drive fails, is lost, or is wiped, your physical backup is the only path to recovering your Bitcoin.

The Passphrase is Integral — A BIP-39 passphrase is not just a password; it is a permanent extension of your seed. Without it, your mnemonic alone cannot recover your funds. Treat the passphrase with the same level of security as the seed itself.

Active Development Notice — SafeKeepVault is currently in active development. We strongly recommend beginning with a nominal balance while you familiarize yourself with the tools. Ensure your physical backups are verified and current before securing significant funds.

System Requirements & Components

SafeKeepVault creates a secure, offline environment without you needing to take your computer apart or buy extra gear. The moment it starts up, it automatically disables all outside connections—like Ethernet, Wi-Fi and storage devices—to keep your data completely isolated.

System Requirements

  • A Boot Drive — A standard USB drive flashed with the SafeKeepVault image. Once you boot your computer from this drive, the system runs in total isolation. Your internal storage remains unmounted and entirely untouched throughout the session.
  • A Host Computer — Any x86-based laptop or desktop with at least 4GB of RAM. SafeKeepVault is engineered to run on a wide range of hardware, regardless of age or performance specifications. No permanent modifications to your hardware or settings are required.
  • Webcam — While not strictly required, an integrated or USB-connected webcam is highly recommended. This enables the use of the Courier Tool, our high-density QR transfer tool, allowing you to sign transactions and verify data without ever physically connecting the vault to another device. It is the most seamless way to maintain a true air-gap.
  • A Password Manager — Strongly recommended for generating and storing the high-entropy password that protects your encrypted vault partition. A password manager lets you use a truly unbreakable credential without the risk of forgetting it, and pairs seamlessly with the QR unlock workflow described in Step 1 below.

Components

  • The Transfer Drive — The USB drive contains a dedicated partition for moving data in and out of the vault. This is the only point of contact between SafeKeepVault and external storage. When combined with the Courier Tool—our high-density QR tool—this creates a secure, bidirectional data flow.
    • Seamless QR Unlocking: By storing your vault password in a manager on your mobile device, you can generate a QR code from that password (see Step 1). Simply display your phone to the host’s webcam to unlock your encrypted vault instantly—combining elite cryptographic strength with a streamlined, convenient way to unlock your wallet.
    • Port Security: Note that for maximum hardware isolation, USB ports are restricted once the vault is active. File-based transfers should be managed by moving the drive between machines while the vault is powered down.

Step 1: Vault Password & QR Generation

Before you boot SafeKeepVault for the first time, you need a vault password ready to go. This password will encrypt the persistent partition of your USB drive—the storage layer where your keys and vault data live between sessions. It is the primary cryptographic barrier protecting everything inside the vault.

Your Password is the Primary Defense

The strength of your vault password is the single most critical security decision you will make during setup. If an unauthorized party gains physical access to your drive, this password is the only barrier protecting your keys. There is no “forgot password” reset and no remote wipe. The password is the absolute lock.

Generate a High-Entropy Password

Open your password manager and generate a new credential for SafeKeepVault. Use a fully random string of at least 20 characters. Do not use a memorable phrase or a password you have used anywhere else. Human-chosen passwords follow predictable patterns that modern brute-force tools can exploit. A randomly generated credential cannot be guessed — only lost. Your password manager is its permanent home; do not write it down in plaintext anywhere.

Generate Your Unlock QR Code

Typing a 20+ character random password on every boot is impractical. Instead, convert it to a QR code using the widget below. Save the QR image to your phone’s secure photo library or an encrypted note in your password manager. On every subsequent boot, simply hold your phone up to the vault’s webcam — the system reads the QR and unlocks the vault instantly, with no typing required. This converter run entirely in your browser - SafeKeepBitcoin does not store your password.

Vault Password → QR Code

Save this image to your phone’s secure photo library or a locked note in your password manager. Display it to the vault’s webcam on every boot to unlock instantly.

Once your QR image is saved, you are ready to move on. Keep your password manager open — you will type this password manually just once during the initial vault setup in Step 3. Every boot after that uses the QR.

Step 2: Download & Image Creation

Download the Software — Visit the Download page to obtain the latest release. The software is provided as a .zip archive; once extracted, you will find the .img disk image required for the installation.

Flash to USB — To write the image to your drive, we recommend using BalenaEtcher, which is available for Windows, macOS, and Linux.

  1. Open BalenaEtcher and select the extracted .img file.
  2. Select your target USB drive.
  3. Click Flash to begin the process.

While BalenaEtcher provides the most straightforward experience across all platforms, advanced users may also utilize the dd utility on macOS/Linux or Rufus on Windows.

Verify the Release — A developer signature is provided to verify software integrity and authenticity. Our Verification Guide contains the public keys and checksums required to validate the release across Windows, macOS, and Linux.

Step 3: Initialization & Encryption

Insert the USB drive, configure your hardware to boot from USB in the UEFI/BIOS settings, and power on. SafeKeepVault skips the usual desktop screen, booting straight into a clean, secure space built only for running the software.

One-Time Configuration: The Encrypted Vault

Upon your initial boot, SafeKeepVault will guide you through the creation of your Encrypted Storage Partition. This persistent volume is where your keys and vault data reside between sessions. When prompted to set your vault password, display the QR code you saved in Step 1 to the webcam. The vault will read it instantly and use it to encrypt the partition. Every subsequent boot works the same way — just scan and you’re in.

Initial Configuration Options

Once the vault is initialized, you are presented with six methods to manage your seed phrase:

  1. Forge a New Seed — Access the Entropy Generator, SafeKeepVault's entropy-driven seed generator. By combining dice rolls, erratic mouse movement, and camera sensor noise, you can generate a master seed with maximum cryptographic randomness. A mandatory verification sequence ensures your physical backup is accurate before any data is committed to the vault.
  2. Restore an Existing Seed — Securely import an existing 12- or 24-word recovery mnemonic.
  3. Seed XOR Operation — Reconstruct a seed phrase by combining multiple backup shares previously split with any Seed XOR tool. The shares are merged exclusively in memory; the individual components are never stored on the drive, maintaining the integrity of your distributed backup scheme.
  4. Shamir Recovery — Utilize the Shamir Backup tool to reconstruct a seed from Shamir’s Secret Sharing (SLIP-39) shares. Simply input the required number of shares to meet your designated threshold, and the vault will restore the seed for immediate use.
  5. Restore from Vault Backup — Import a previously encrypted vault archive from your transfer interface. This restores your entire environment—including all stored keys, passphrases, and custom configurations—onto a new or freshly flashed boot drive.
  6. Temporary (Stateless) Session — Boot into a volatile session without writing any data to the physical drive. Manually enter your seed, perform the necessary operations, and shut down to purge all traces instantly. This stateless mode is ideal for users who prefer to keep their keys exclusively on physical media.

Step 4: Mounting the Transfer Drive

Once your seed is loaded and you land on the Dashboard, your next step is to mount the Transfer Drive. This is the dedicated partition on your USB drive used to move files in and out of the vault — signed transactions, PSBT files, encrypted backups, and exported reports all pass through here.

Do this on every boot

The Transfer Drive is not mounted automatically. You must mount it manually at the start of each session. Tools that require file access will not function until it is mounted.

How to Mount

  1. Click “Mount Transfer Drive” — On the Dashboard, you will see a prominent Mount Transfer Drive button. Click it. This opens the system file explorer automatically.
  2. Select the Transfer Partition — In the file explorer, you will see your USB drive listed with two partitions. Select the one labelled Transfer (not the system partition).
  3. Click Allow — A permission prompt will appear asking whether to allow access to the partition. Click Allow. The Transfer Drive will mount and become available to all tools immediately.

Once mounted, every tool in the vault can read from and write to the Transfer Drive automatically. The status indicator in the toolbar will confirm when it is live. You only need to mount it once per session.

Step 5: Watch Wallet Creation

Your vault holds the private keys; a watch-only wallet on your everyday device holds the public keys. The watch wallet tracks your balances, generates receive addresses, and builds unsigned transactions — all without ever touching a private key. When it’s time to spend, the watch wallet hands off an unsigned PSBT to the vault for signing, then broadcasts the result.

To set up a watch wallet, you first need to export your public key or output descriptor from the vault. Open the Seed Management tool inside SafeKeepVault, select your desired script type (Native SegWit, Taproot, etc.), and export the descriptor or extended public key. The export lands on your Transfer Drive as a file, or can be transmitted directly via QR using the Courier Tool. Transfer it to your online device and follow the setup steps for your chosen wallet below.

Blue Wallet (Mobile — iOS & Android)

Blue Wallet is the simplest mobile option for balance monitoring and receiving funds. It imports both single-signature and multisig watch wallets directly via QR code from the vault — no file transfer required.

Single Signature

SafeKeepVault — Seed Management export settings Key Format: SLIP-380 Wallet Type: Single-Sig
  1. Tap Add Wallet and choose type Bitcoin.
  2. Tap Import Wallet.
  3. Tap Scan or import a file.
  4. Scan the QR code displayed on SafeKeepVault. Blue Wallet imports the xpub and fingerprint automatically and creates your watch wallet.

Multisig

SafeKeepVault — Seed Management export settings (repeat for each co-signer key) Key Format: SLIP-380 Wallet Type: Multi-Sig (BIP-48)
  1. Tap Add Wallet and choose type Multisig Vault.
  2. Tap Create, then Let’s Start.
  3. Tap Import vault key 1 → Scan or import a file.
  4. Scan the first key QR from SafeKeepVault.
  5. Repeat steps 3–4 for each additional co-signer key.
  6. Tap Create to finalize the multisig vault.

Nunchuk (Mobile — iOS & Android)

Nunchuk is the recommended mobile option for an active signing workflow. It imports keys via a Hardware Wallet JSON file transferred from the vault using the Courier Tool.

Single Signature

SafeKeepVault — Seed Management export settings Wallet Type: Single-Sig Key Format: Hardware Wallet JSON
  1. In SafeKeepVault, open Seed Management, configure the settings above, and tap Download JSON. The file saves to your Transfer Drive.
  2. Open the Transfer Hub in the vault and locate the JSON file you just downloaded.
  3. On your phone, open the Courier Tool and select Receive, then tap Open camera and scan.
  4. On SafeKeepVault, select the JSON file and tap Transmit → Start Transmission. The vault displays an animated QR sequence.
  5. Hold your phone to the vault’s screen. The Courier Tool will scan and reconstruct the file. Save it to your phone when prompted.
  6. In Nunchuk, tap Add Key → Coldcard → Add COLDCARD via file (advanced) → Continue.
  7. Navigate to the JSON file you transferred and select it.
  8. Name your key and tap Add Key. Your signing key is now registered.
  9. Return to the Nunchuk home screen and tap + to add a wallet.
  10. Select Custom Wallet, name it, choose the address type you used in step 1, and tap Continue.
  11. Select the key you created in step 8, tap Continue, then Create Wallet.

Multisig

SafeKeepVault — Seed Management export settings (repeat for each co-signer key) Wallet Type: Multi-Sig (BIP-48) Key Format: Hardware Wallet JSON
  1. Follow the same key creation steps as Single Signature above, selecting Multi-Sig (BIP-48) as the Wallet Type in SafeKeepVault. Repeat for each co-signer key.
  2. In Nunchuk, tap + and select Group Wallet.
  3. Select the appropriate co-signer keys you created, configure the signing threshold (e.g., 2-of-3), and tap Create Wallet.

Sparrow (Desktop — Windows, macOS, Linux)

Sparrow is the recommended desktop coordinator for SafeKeepVault. Setup is file-based: export a Hardware Wallet JSON from the vault and open it directly in Sparrow — no descriptor syntax or manual key entry required.

Getting the file from the vault to your computer requires one of two approaches. You can move the USB drive directly to your online machine (sneakernet), but note that removing the drive from a live vault session requires a full reboot before the drive can be re-inserted. The simpler alternative is to use the Courier Tool to transfer the file to your phone, then share it to your computer via a cloud service such as Google Drive.

Single Signature

SafeKeepVault — Seed Management export settings Wallet Type: Single-Sig Key Format: Hardware Wallet JSON
  1. In SafeKeepVault, open Seed Management, configure the settings above, and tap Download JSON. The file saves to your Transfer Drive.
  2. Transfer the JSON file to your online computer using sneakernet or the phone → cloud method described above.
  3. Open Sparrow and go to File → Open Wallet.
  4. Navigate to and select the JSON file you transferred. Sparrow reads the key data and populates the wallet configuration automatically.
  5. Click Create Wallet to save.
  6. Sparrow will sync and display your balance and transaction history.

Multisig

SafeKeepVault — Seed Management export settings (repeat for each co-signer key) Wallet Type: Multi-Sig (BIP-48) Key Format: SLIP-132
  1. In SafeKeepVault, open Seed Management, configure the settings above, and tap Download JSON. Repeat for each co-signer key. Transfer all files to your online computer using sneakernet or the phone → cloud method described above.
  2. Open Sparrow and go to File → New Wallet. Give your wallet a name.
  3. Select Multi Signature and configure your cosigner quorum (e.g., 2-of-3).
  4. For the first keystore, click xPub / Watch Only Wallet, then click Import → Airgapped Hardware Wallet → Import File → Coldcard Multisig. Navigate to and select the first key file.
  5. Repeat step 4 for each remaining keystore, selecting the corresponding key file each time.
  6. Once all keys are loaded, click Apply to complete the wallet.

Specter Desktop (Desktop — Windows, macOS, Linux)

Specter Desktop is a strong choice for both single-signature and multisig coordination. Like Sparrow, setup is file-based — export from the vault, transfer the file to your computer via sneakernet or the phone → cloud method, and import directly into Specter.

Single Signature

SafeKeepVault — Seed Management export settings Wallet Type: Single-Sig Key Format: Hardware Wallet JSON
  1. In SafeKeepVault, open Seed Management, configure the settings above, and tap Download JSON. The file saves to your Transfer Drive.
  2. Transfer the JSON file to your online computer using sneakernet or the phone → cloud method described above.
  3. Open Specter Desktop and click Add Device.
  4. Select device type Other, then click Upload from SD to import your xpub. Navigate to and select the JSON file, then click Continue.
  5. Specter confirms the device has been added. Click Create Single Key Wallet.
  6. Give your wallet a name and click Continue to complete setup.

Electrum (Desktop — Windows, macOS, Linux)

SafeKeepVault generates a purpose-built Electrum skeleton wallet file directly from the Seed Management tool. This file contains the correct descriptor, derivation path, and ckcc_xfp field for instant watch-only import.

  1. In the vault, open Seed Management and export the [FINGERPRINT]-electrum.json file to your Transfer Drive.
  2. Transfer the file to your online machine and open Electrum.
  3. Go to File → Open and select the exported .json file. Electrum will load it as a watch-only wallet immediately.
  4. To sign a PSBT, go to Tools → Load Transaction → From File, select your unsigned PSBT, and click Export → As QR Code to transmit it to the vault.
  5. After signing inside the vault, use the Courier Tool to scan the signed result back into Electrum and broadcast.

The Tool Arsenal

Fifteen tools are included. Every one runs completely offline and works with any Bitcoin wallet that supports standard formats.

Primary Tools

  • PSBT Signer — Reviews, decodes, and signs PSBTs entirely offline. Transaction details are displayed for inspection before you sign.
  • Passphrase Library — Manages BIP-39 passphrases (the “25th word”) inside the encrypted vault. Loads directly into the PSBT Signer when signing requires a passphrase-protected wallet.
  • Seed Management — Exports your public key and output descriptor for watch-only wallet setup in Sparrow, Electrum, Nunchuk, Blue Wallet, Specter, or any compatible coordinator. Your private keys never leave.

Advanced Tools

  • Transfer Hub — File browser for the Transfer Drive. View, rename, or delete backup archives, transactions, and PDFs.
  • Courier Tool — Transfers files between air-gapped devices via animated QR codes. No USB, no network — light and a camera lens only.
  • Output Descriptor — Builds wallet configurations for single-signature and multisig setups. Export and import directly into any compatible wallet.
  • Entropy Generator — Generates your master seed using multiple sources of randomness: dice rolls, mouse movement, camera noise, and the system’s built-in random number generator.
  • BIP-85 Child Seeds — Generates separate child wallets from your master seed — isolated wallets for different purposes, all recoverable from one backup.
  • Seed XOR Splitter — Splits your seed into XOR shares for distributed cold storage. Reconstructs your master seed by combining these shares.
  • Shamir Backup — Splits your seed into Shamir shares for distributed backup. Recovery requires only a portion of the shares (2-of-3, 3-of-5, etc.) to restore.
  • Seed QR Code — Displays a QR encoded version of a seed as a compact SeedQR for steel stamping or paper backup. Hand-drawn or engraved copies can be verified by scanning them back in. Blank templates are also available for printing.
  • Message Signer — BIP-322 message signing. Proves address ownership with a verifiable proof without moving any coins.
  • Encrypted Notes — Encrypted notes stored inside the vault. Keep recovery instructions, multisig details, and setup notes sealed within the vault. For your eyes only.
  • Encrypted Backup — Exports a fully encrypted backup of your vault to the transfer drive. Everything inside is sealed — file names included — until the correct password is entered.
  • Wallet Record — Generates a clean, single-page PDF summary of your wallet configuration — descriptor, derivation path, key fingerprint, and first receive address — formatted for long-term offline storage and inheritance planning.
