Getting Started

Everything you need to go from a blank USB drive to signing your first transaction, start to finish.

Critical Precautions: Read Before Proceeding

Your 12- or 24-word recovery mnemonic is the only unconditional backup of your Bitcoin.

Physical Backups are Mandatory — Transcribe your seed words and BIP-39 passphrase onto physical media (paper or steel) and store them in separate, secure locations. Never store these records digitally. If your USB drive fails, is lost, or is wiped, your physical backup is the singular path to recovering your assets.

The Passphrase is Integral — A BIP-39 passphrase is not just a password; it is a permanent extension of your seed. Without it, your mnemonic alone cannot recover your funds. Treat the passphrase with the same level of security as the seed itself.

Active Development Notice — SafeKeepVault is currently in active development. We strongly recommend beginning with a nominal balance while you familiarize yourself with the air-gapped workflow. Ensure your physical backups are verified and current before securing significant capital.

Step 1: Download & Image Creation

Retrieve the Software — Visit the Download page to obtain the latest release. The software is provided as a .zip archive; once extracted, you will find the .img disk image required for the installation.

Flash to USB — To write the image to your drive, we recommend using BalenaEtcher, which is available for Windows, macOS, and Linux.

  1. Open BalenaEtcher and select the extracted .img file.
  2. Select your target USB drive.
  3. Click Flash to begin the process.

While BalenaEtcher provides the most straightforward experience across all platforms, advanced users may also utilize the dd utility on macOS/Linux or Rufus on Windows.

Verify the Release — Before flashing, we strongly advise verifying the download against the developer’s cryptographic signature. This ensures the integrity and authenticity of your software. Our Verification Guide provides step-by-step instructions for Windows, macOS, and Linux users to confirm their download is secure.

Step 2: System Requirements & Components

SafeKeepVault establishes a robust software-level air-gap without requiring physical hardware modifications or specialized networking equipment. The system automatically severs all network and Bluetooth interfaces upon initialization. To begin, you will need the following three components:

  1. The Boot Drive — A standard USB drive flashed with the SafeKeepVault image. Once you boot your computer from this drive, the environment initializes in total isolation. Your internal storage remains unmounted and entirely untouched throughout the session.
  2. The Offline Host — Any x86-based laptop or desktop. SafeKeepVault is engineered to run on a wide range of hardware, regardless of age or performance specifications. Because the “network lockout” is enforced automatically at the kernel level during startup, no permanent modifications to your hardware or settings are required.
    • The Role of the Webcam: While not strictly required, an integrated or USB-connected webcam is highly recommended. This enables the use of the Courier Tool, our high-density QR tool, allowing you to sign transactions and verify data without ever physically connecting the vault to another device. It is the most seamless way to maintain a true “zero-contact” air-gap.
  3. The Transfer & Access Interface — The boot drive contains a dedicated partition for bridging the air-gap between environments. When combined with the Courier Tool—our high-density QR tool—this creates a secure, bidirectional data flow.
    • Optimized Access via Password Managers: For the highest level of security, we strongly recommend using a password manager to generate and store a high-entropy, “unbreakable” password for your encrypted storage. This ensures you maintain a robust defense without the risk of forgetting complex credentials.
    • Seamless QR Unlocking: By storing your vault password in a manager on your mobile device, you can use the Courier Tool to transform that password into a QR code. Simply present your phone to the host’s webcam to unlock your encrypted vault instantly—combining elite cryptographic strength with a streamlined, air-gapped workflow.
    • Port Security: Note that for maximum hardware isolation, USB ports are restricted once the vault is active. File-based transfers should be managed by moving the drive between machines while the vault is powered down.

Step 3: Initialization & Encryption

Insert the USB drive, configure your hardware to boot from USB in the UEFI/BIOS settings, and power on. SafeKeepVault bypasses the standard desktop experience, loading directly into a distraction-free, hardened environment.

One-Time Configuration: The Encrypted Vault

Upon your initial boot, SafeKeepVault will guide you through the creation of your Encrypted Storage Partition. This persistent volume is where your keys and vault data reside between sessions.

Your Password is the Primary Defense

The entropy of your vault password is the most critical security decision you will make. While SafeKeepVault provides a hardened air-gap and auditable code, these defenses rely entirely on the strength of your password. If an unauthorized party gains physical access to your drive, your password is the only barrier protecting your keys. There is no “forgot password” reset and no remote wipe; the password is the absolute lock.

The Professional Workflow:

  • Generate, Don’t Invent: Use a password manager to generate a high-entropy, fully random string of at least 20 characters. Human-chosen passwords often follow predictable patterns that modern brute-force tools can exploit.
  • The Courier Integration: Once your password is generated, use the Courier Tool to convert it into a QR code. Save this code securely on your mobile device.
  • Instant Authentication: For every subsequent boot, simply present the QR code to your webcam to unlock the vault. This eliminates the inconvenience of manual entry and allows you to utilize maximum-strength passwords without the burden of typing them.

Initial Configuration Options

Once the vault is initialized, you are presented with six professional-grade workflows to manage your keys:

  1. Forge a New Seed — Access the Entropy Generator, SafeKeepVault’s entropy-driven seed generator. By synthesizing dice rolls, erratic mouse movement, and camera sensor noise, you can generate a master seed with maximum cryptographic randomness. A mandatory verification sequence ensures your physical backup is accurate before any data is committed to the vault.
  2. Restore an Existing Seed — Securely import an existing 12- or 24-word recovery mnemonic to bring your current keys into the hardened SafeKeepVault environment.
  3. Seed XOR Operation — Reconstruct a master seed by combining multiple backup shares previously split with any Seed XOR tool. These shares are merged exclusively in volatile memory; the individual components are never stored on the drive, maintaining the integrity of your distributed backup scheme.
  4. Shamir Recovery — Utilize the Shamir Backup tool to reconstruct a seed from Shamir’s Secret Sharing (SLIP-39) shares. Simply input the required number of shares to meet your designated threshold, and the vault will restore the seed for immediate use.
  5. Restore from Vault Backup — Import a previously encrypted vault archive from your transfer interface. This restores your entire environment—including all stored keys, metadata, and custom configurations—onto a new or freshly flashed boot drive.
  6. Temporary (Stateless) Session — Execute a purely volatile session without writing any data to the physical drive. Manually enter your seed, perform the necessary operations, and shut down to purge all traces instantly. This “stateless” mode is ideal for high-privacy signing sessions or users who prefer to keep their keys exclusively on physical media.

Once your seed is loaded, you'll land on the Dashboard. Click Mount Transfer Drive to connect the built-in transfer folder on your thumb drive — this is how unsigned transactions, signed transactions, reports, and backups move in and out. Mount it once per session and every tool picks it up automatically. The status light in the toolbar turns green when it's live.

Prefer to never plug your drive into an online computer? Use The Courier tool alongside the live Courier tool on this site. Transactions cross the air-gap as QR codes only — no shared drive, no cable, no radio.

The Tool Arsenal

Fifteen tools ship in v1.1b, organized under four working sections. Every one runs completely offline and works with any Bitcoin wallet that supports standard formats.

Transaction

  • PSBT Signer — Reviews, decodes, and signs PSBTs entirely offline. Every input and output is displayed for inspection before the signature is committed.
  • Message Signer — BIP-322 message signing. Proves address ownership with a verifiable proof without moving any coins.

Seed Management

  • Entropy Generator — Generates your master seed using multiple sources of randomness: dice rolls, mouse movement, camera noise, and the system’s built-in random number generator. No single source is trusted on its own.
  • Passphrase Library — Manages BIP-39 passphrases (the “25th word”) inside the encrypted vault. Loads directly into the PSBT Signer when signing requires a passphrase-protected wallet.
  • Seed Management — Exports your public key for watch-only wallet setup in Sparrow, Electrum, or any compatible wallet. Your private keys never leave.
  • Output Descriptor — Builds wallet configurations for single-signature and multisig setups. Export and import directly into any compatible wallet.
  • Shamir Backup — Splits your seed into Shamir shares for distributed backup. Recovery requires only a portion of the shares (2-of-3, 3-of-5, etc.) — any single share alone reveals nothing.
  • Seed XOR Splitter — Splits your seed into XOR shares for distributed cold storage. Unlike Shamir, every share is needed to recover — losing one means losing access.
  • BIP-85 Child Seeds — Generates separate child wallets from your master seed — isolated wallets for different purposes, all recoverable from one backup.

Backup & Verification

  • Seed QR Code — Encodes a seed as a compact SeedQR for steel stamping or paper backup. Hand-drawn or engraved copies can be verified by scanning them back in.
  • Courier Tool — Transfers PSBTs and descriptors between air-gapped devices via animated QR codes (BCR-UR / BBQr). No USB, no network — light and a camera lens only.
  • Transfer Hub — File browser for the Transfer Drive. View, rename, or delete backup archives and signed transactions without leaving the air-gap.
  • Encrypted Backup — Exports a fully encrypted backup of your vault to the transfer drive. Everything inside is sealed — file names included — until the correct password is entered.

Workspace

  • Encrypted Notes — Encrypted notes stored inside the vault. Keep recovery instructions, multisig details, and setup notes sealed alongside your seed.
  • Inheritance Map — Produces a technical map of your custody setup: derivation paths, account public keys, and script types an heir needs to reconstruct the wallet. Print it and store it with your physical inheritance plan.

That summarizes the complete workflow: generate your entropy, secure your physical backups, integrate with your preferred coordinator, and sign transactions in total isolation.

Zero connectivity. Zero shortcuts. Zero compromise.
