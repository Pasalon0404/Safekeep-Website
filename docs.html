Getting Started

Everything you need to go from a blank USB drive to signing your first transaction, start to finish.

Before you begin — read this

Write your seed words and passphrase on paper and store them offline. Your 12- or 24-word mnemonic is the only unconditional backup of your Bitcoin. A BIP-39 passphrase is equally critical — a seed without it cannot recover your funds. Write both separately, store them safely, and never store either digitally. If your vault drive fails or is lost, your written backup is the only way back.

SafeKeepVault is in active development. Start with a small amount while you get comfortable with the workflow, and always keep your written seed backup current.

Step 1: Download & Flash

Head to the Download page to grab the latest release. It comes as a .zip file — unzip it and you'll find the .img disk image inside. That’s the file you’ll write to your thumb drive.

The easiest way to flash it is with Balena Etcher. Open Etcher, select the .img file, select your thumb drive, and hit Flash. It takes a few minutes and tells you when it's done. If you prefer the command line, you can also use dd on macOS or Linux.

Before flashing, it’s worth taking two minutes to verify the download against the developer’s signature. The verification guide on the Download page walks you through it step by step.

Step 2: What You Need

SafeKeepVault creates an air-gap in software — no physical modifications, no special networking equipment. It cuts off internet and Bluetooth at startup automatically. You just need three things:

  • The Boot Drive — A USB thumb drive flashed with the SafeKeepVault image. Plug it in, start the computer from it, and you're in the vault. Your internal hard drive is never touched.
  • An Offline Machine — Any x86 laptop or desktop — doesn't need to be new or powerful. SafeKeepVault handles the internet lockout automatically at startup. No changes to your computer required.
  • The Transfer Folder — The thumb drive includes a built-in folder for moving files in and out. This and The Courier QR tool are the only ways to get data across. Once the vault is running, the USB ports lock down, so move the drive between machines while the vault is powered off.

Step 3: First Boot

Plug in the thumb drive, set your computer to boot from USB in its startup settings, and power on. SafeKeepVault loads straight into the vault — no desktop, no distractions, just your tools.

One-Time Setup: Encrypted Storage

The very first time you boot, SafeKeepVault walks you through a quick setup: creating your encrypted storage. This is where your seed and vault data live between sessions. You'll pick a password to protect it.

Your password is your security

The strength of your encrypted drive password is the single most important security decision you will make in SafeKeepVault. The air-gap, offline tools, open-source code — none of it matters if your partition can be brute-forced. Anyone with physical access to your boot drive has access to your keys. There is no remote wipe, no second factor. The password is the lock. Make it unbreakable.

Use a password manager to generate a long, fully random password — at least 20 characters. Do not invent one yourself. Human-chosen passwords are predictable in ways that matter to an attacker even when they feel random to you.

Once you have your password, use The Courier to turn it into a QR code and save a screenshot to your phone. From then on, just hold your phone up to the camera at startup and you're in — no typing required. This also removes the temptation to use a simpler password because it's easier to type. This whole setup happens once. Every boot after that goes straight to the unlock prompt.

First Boot Options

Once you're in, you'll see six options to get started:

  • Forge a New Seed — opens The Forge, SafeKeepVault's seed generator. Mix dice rolls, mouse movement, and camera noise together to generate your master seed. A verification quiz walks you through confirming your backup before anything gets saved.
  • Restore an Existing Seed — type in your 12- or 24-word seed phrase to load a seed you already have.
  • Seed XOR — load a seed by combining two or more backup shares that were split with The Shard tool. The shares are combined in memory and never stored individually.
  • Shamir Recovery — reconstruct a seed from Shamir backup shares. Enter enough shares to meet your threshold and The Covenant tool loads it into the vault.
  • Restore from Backup — load a previously encrypted vault backup from your transfer drive. Use this to recover your full vault state, including any stored keys and configuration, onto a new or re-flashed boot drive.
  • Ephemeral Session — run the vault without saving anything. Type in your seed phrase, use the tools you need, and when you shut down it's gone — nothing written to the drive, no trace left behind. This is the 'stateless' mode: great for one-off signing sessions or for those who prefer to keep their seed phrase on paper only and enter it fresh each time.

Once your seed is loaded, you'll land on the Dashboard. Click Mount Transfer Drive to connect the built-in transfer folder on your thumb drive — this is how unsigned transactions, signed transactions, reports, and backups move in and out. Mount it once per session and every tool picks it up automatically. The status light in the toolbar turns green when it's live.

Prefer to never plug your drive into an online computer? Use The Courier tool alongside the live Courier tool on this site. Transactions cross the air-gap as QR codes only — no shared drive, no cable, no radio.

The Tool Arsenal

Fifteen tools ship in v1.1b, organized under four working sections. Every one runs completely offline and works with any Bitcoin wallet that supports standard formats.

Transaction

  • The Scribe — Reviews, decodes, and signs PSBTs entirely offline. Every input and output is displayed for inspection before the signature is committed.
  • The Witness — BIP-322 message signing. Proves address ownership with a verifiable proof without moving any coins.

Seed Management

  • The Forge — Generates your master seed using multiple sources of randomness: dice rolls, mouse movement, camera noise, and the system's built-in random number generator. No single source is trusted on its own.
  • The Cipher — Manages BIP-39 passphrases (the “25th word”) inside the encrypted vault. Loads directly into The Scribe when signing requires a passphrase-protected wallet.
  • The Armory — Exports your public key for watch-only wallet setup in Sparrow, Electrum, or any compatible wallet. Your private keys never leave.
  • The Script — Builds wallet configurations for single-signature and multisig setups. Export and import directly into any compatible wallet.
  • The Covenant — Splits your seed into Shamir shares for distributed backup. Recovery requires only a portion of the shares (2-of-3, 3-of-5, etc.) — any single share alone reveals nothing.
  • The Shard — Splits your seed into XOR shares for distributed cold storage. Unlike Shamir, every share is needed to recover — losing one means losing access.
  • The Lineage — Generates separate child wallets from your master seed — isolated wallets for different purposes, all recoverable from one backup.

Backup & Verification

  • The Glyph — Encodes a seed as a compact SeedQR for steel stamping or paper backup. Hand-drawn or engraved copies can be verified by scanning them back in.
  • The Courier — Transfers PSBTs and descriptors between air-gapped devices via animated QR codes (BCR-UR / BBQr). No USB, no network — light and a camera lens only.
  • The Archive — File browser for the Transfer Drive. View, rename, or delete backup archives and signed transactions without leaving the air-gap.
  • The Reliquary — Exports a fully encrypted backup of your vault to the transfer drive. Everything inside is sealed — file names included — until the correct password is entered.

Workspace

  • The Codex — Encrypted notes stored inside the vault. Keep recovery instructions, multisig details, and setup notes sealed alongside your seed.
  • The Cartographer — Produces a technical map of your custody setup: derivation paths, account public keys, and script types an heir needs to reconstruct the wallet. Print it and store it with your physical inheritance plan.

That’s the whole workflow: generate your seed, back it up, connect it to your everyday wallet, sign transactions offline, and send the results back. No internet. No shortcuts. No compromise.
