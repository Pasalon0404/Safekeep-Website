About SafeKeepVault

Built Because It
Should Exist

Self-custody shouldn’t cost $150 and a leap of faith in a manufacturer you’ve never met.

The Problem with How Most People Hold Bitcoin

Most Bitcoin sits on exchanges. The people who have taken the step to self-custody have largely done so through dedicated hardware wallets — small, proprietary devices with tiny screens, closed firmware, and supply chains you cannot verify. You are trusting the manufacturer not to have introduced vulnerabilities, not to have been compromised, and not to go out of business before you need to recover your funds.

That is a significant amount of trust to place in a piece of hardware for something as consequential as your Bitcoin. And the smaller the screen, the harder it is to actually read and verify what you are signing before you sign it.

A Different Approach

SafeKeepVault starts from a different premise: the hardware you already own is probably powerful enough, and the real problem has never been the hardware — it has been the software environment running on it.

A Windows or macOS machine is not safe for key operations. Not because the hardware is bad, but because it is always online, always running background processes, and always reachable. SafeKeepVault solves that by replacing the environment entirely at boot. Plug in the drive, restart the machine, and it comes up as a stripped-down, network-disabled, read-only signing station — with your full-size screen, your keyboard, and hardware you already paid for.

No supply chain to trust. No closed firmware. No manufacturer account. No tiny screen squinting at a derivation path you can barely read.

What We Believe

  • Self-custody is a right, not a premium feature — the tools to hold your own Bitcoin securely should be free, open, and accessible to anyone willing to learn how to use them.
  • Trust should be minimised, not shifted — every component of SafeKeepVault is open source and auditable. You should not have to take our word for anything. Read the code, verify the release signature, and run it yourself.
  • Transparency is the only real security guarantee — security through obscurity is not security. We follow open Bitcoin standards, produce human-readable output, and make every derivation path and transaction detail visible and verifiable before you sign.
  • Your hardware should work for you — the laptop collecting dust in a drawer is a capable signing machine. You already paid for it. SafeKeepVault gives it a job.

Fully Open Source

SafeKeepVault is MIT licensed. The full source is on GitHub, the release is signed with a developer key you can verify, and the SHA-256 hash of every release is published so you can confirm you are running exactly what we shipped.

We are a small project and we welcome scrutiny. If you find a bug, a vulnerability, or something that does not look right, open an issue. If you want to contribute, open a pull request. The goal is software that earns trust through transparency, not through marketing.
