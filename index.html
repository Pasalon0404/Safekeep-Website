Begin Your Self-Custody Journey

Bitcoin self-custody isn't purchased—it's learned. Owning your keys is the foundation, and the tools to do it right should be accessible to anyone willing to begin, whether protecting a small amount or a lifetime of savings. SafeKeepVault helps you move from decision to confident signature.

SafeKeepVault is built to work with the Bitcoin tools that already exist. It follows the same standards, speaks the same formats, and fits cleanly into established workflows—whether you're importing descriptors, exporting xpubs, signing PSBTs, or moving data by QR.

What we add is a full-featured signer that runs on hardware you already own. A first vault for the newcomer, a travel signer for the veteran, a practice environment for the curious, a backup signer for a multisig quorum. The vault is a stepping stone into the ecosystem and a companion once you are inside it—whichever of those you need, it is yours, and it is free.

Repurpose, Don't Replace

There is almost certainly a laptop in your closet that has not been powered on in two years. Plug in a USB thumb drive with SafeKeepVault, boot, and that old machine becomes a secure, air-gapped, full-featured Bitcoin wallet. SafeKeepVault turns dormant hardware into sovereign infrastructure.

The ergonomic payoff is immediate. Instead of pecking at a four-line OLED, you get a full-size keyboard under your fingers and a full laptop display for the transaction you are about to sign. Every input and output is legible at a glance; every derivation path fits on one screen. Critical security operations stop feeling like a chore and start feeling like work you can actually double-check.

The Fortress

The OS is engineered to make the host machine a hostile environment for anything it doesn't explicitly trust. Every layer is a deliberate choice—security not as friction, but as the state the system returns to every time you boot.

  • Zero network All networking and Bluetooth are disabled at the kernel level during the boot sequence, creating a pristine, verifiable air-gap.
  • Disabled peripherals Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, microphones, and speakers stay cold. The vault speaks only to the keyboard, display, camera, and transfer drive.
  • Host SSD untouched The internal NVMe or SATA drive is ignored entirely. Nothing on the host disk can be read, written, or contaminated.
  • Amnesic sandbox Runs entirely in RAM. At power-down, the entire working state—seeds, PSBTs, clipboards, memory buffers—is permanently destroyed. Zero footprint.
  • Plausible deniability No branded device, no custom hardware in a drawer. The vault is a thumb drive that looks exactly like any other thumb drive.
  • Flexible unlock Decrypt the encrypted volume with a QR code scanned from your phone or a strong password at the keyboard—whichever suits your setup.
  • Encrypted or ephemeral Save seed words to the encrypted vault for repeated use, or run the whole session in pure amnesic mode and leave nothing behind. The same OS does both.

What’s New in v1.1b

v1.1b conquers air-gap interoperability for the two strictest mobile and desktop wallet implementations in the ecosystem: Nunchuk and Electrum. Workflows that previously required manual conversion steps now complete in a single scan.

v1.1b
  • Compat Flawless Nunchuk integration. SafeKeep outputs pure, un-prefixed Raw Hex QR codes. Nunchuk scans and broadcasts without any intermediate conversion step.
  • Compat Complete Electrum round-trip. A custom Base43 decoder built into SafeKeep’s camera reads Electrum’s proprietary PSBT format natively — the last manual step is gone.
  • New Electrum skeleton wallet export. SafeKeep generates a [FINGERPRINT]-electrum.json file with the correct descriptor, derivation path, and little-endian ckcc_xfp field for instant watch-only wallet creation.
